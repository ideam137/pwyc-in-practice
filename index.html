PWYC in Practice


Component 1: Access Type

Gym access falls into two general categories: day passes (to include punch passes) and monthly memberships. Either pass can be scaled for PWYC, and they are not necessarily mutually exclusive. In other words, a gym could choose to offer both PWYC day passes and monthly memberships, although we recommend gyms carefully consider if there will be any financial implications of offering both access types in PWYC varieties simultaneously.






Component 3: Financial Support

Offering reduced memberships does not guarantee that a gym loses money on these memberships. Most PWYC participants use the program because they otherwise couldn't afford a “standard” rate, meaning that PWYC revenue is revenue the gym would not be receiving if they did not offer alternative payment models. However, PWYC programs do incur costs: staff time to design and implement programming, offering amenities to additional members, etc. Additionally, some owners are concerned about “closing the gap” between a PWYC rate and a “standard” rate, although we found this was oftentimes a perception issue rather than a true financial concern when considering consumer intent.
